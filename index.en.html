About me

With more presence. With more attention to what the body says before it speaks.

Movement was always there. Tennis, padel, basketball, and other sports over the years. The body has always been where I start to understand what actually works.

I spent years working in engineering and consulting, in multinational environments. Fast pace, long hours, and a lot of time sitting. Tension builds slowly in that rhythm. Posture shifts. The body adapts, quietly, until it doesn't anymore.

I came to Thai Yoga Massage through yoga. I trained in Lisbon and later went to Chiang Mai, in the north of Thailand, where the practice is still taught in its original form. I came back with a different sense of touch, and a different way of listening.

Today I work with people whose patterns I know. Professionals with little time. People who push their body, but don't always give back.

Tennis is asymmetric by nature. That asymmetry builds quietly until it shows up as pain or restriction. I know this because I live it.

In each session, the intention is simple: you leave with more space in your body than when you arrived.

The massage

Each session is
a ritual of presence.

We work on the floor, on a comfortable futon, allowing more space for movement and better leverage for stretching.

·

Rhythmic touch

Soft and rhythmic pressure along the body, which can become deeper depending on what the moment asks for.

·

Mobilization

Assisted stretches, similar to passive yoga postures. You let go, I guide.

·

No rush

The time of the session is respected so the nervous system can truly lower its guard and allow deeper tension to ease.

"A sense of space and inner quiet that stays with you."
